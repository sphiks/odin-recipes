Pan-Seared Chicken!

This chicken recipe is so simple you won't believe me until you try it. With only four ingredients, high heat, and 10-15 minutes, you will have the tastiest, juiciest chicken you never thought possible. The best part? It's incredibly versatile. If you want to add more spices than salt and pepper, go for it! If you want to make a sauce to dip it in, be my guest! And if you want to marinate it and make it even more succulent, then you better invite me over for dinner!

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-large chicken breast
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil

Steps:

  1. Gather your ingredients and slice chicken breast into even halves. (you'll want this to be as even as possible to make an even cooking area)
  2. Cover both sides of the chicken with a generous helping of salt and pepper. (put as much or as little salt as you like, but remember that most people undersalt their chicken 😉)
  3. Heat 2 tbsp of oil on a high heat.
  4. Just before the oil starts to smoke, i.e., burn, add the chicken to the pan and do not touch it, fiddle with it, or monkey with it in any way! Leave it for about 3-4 minutes
  5. Flip the chicken over to the other side and lower the heat to medium to avoid undercooking the middle. The top of your chicken should have a nice browning color but not burnt. If youre chicken looks light brown, flip it back over for a minute or two, if it's burnt then pray that it still tastes good
  6. After 6-8 minutes, check the temperature of the chicken. A safe temperature to aim for is 165 degrees farenheit.
  7. Once you are at or around that temperature, remove from heat and let rest for 1-2 minutes before cutting into it
  8. You're done! If you followed this recipe to the T, pat yourself on the back and enjoy your unseasoned chicken.
